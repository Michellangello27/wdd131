Cultural & Social Benefits

Peruvian handicrafts are more than objects. They connect people, protect traditions, generate income and create opportunities for sustainable development.

Across the Andes, the coast and the Amazon, artisans transform local materials into unique pieces that preserve memory and support entire families. This page highlights the main cultural, social and economic benefits of Peruvian handicrafts.

Handicrafts strengthen cultural identity by keeping ancestral knowledge, symbols and worldviews alive. Designs, colors and motifs tell stories about the land, local myths and everyday life. For many communities, creating and using these objects is a way to feel proud of who they are and where they come from.

Traditional techniques such as pottery, backstrap weaving, silver filigree and wood carving are passed from one generation to the next. Workshops and family ateliers act as living archives where patterns, tools and processes are carefully preserved, adapted and shared with younger artisans.

Craft production generates direct income for thousands of families in rural and urban areas. Sales in fairs, local markets, museums and online platforms help artisans diversify their income, invest in tools and education, and reduce migration by creating opportunities within their own communities.
