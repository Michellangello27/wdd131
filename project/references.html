References

The following references and sources were consulted to prepare the educational content, structure and examples used in the Peruvian Handicrafts Guide project.

  • General background information about Peruvian regions, culture and handicrafts was summarized from personal study, course materials, and publicly available tourism and cultural promotion resources from Peru.
  • Descriptions of techniques such as ceramics, textiles, wood carving and silver filigree were inspired by common characteristics of traditional Andean, coastal and Amazonian crafts observed in educational articles, museum exhibits and artisan fairs.
  • The structure of the site (home, techniques, history, benefits, gallery and contact) follows best practices learned in the BYU–Idaho WDD 131 course for planning and building small educational web projects.
  • Fonts: Libre Baskerville and Open Sans, served via Google Fonts.
  • Images and illustrations:
      Many reference images were first collected through Google searches to better understand compositions and styles.
      Custom compositions were created based on the indicated websites, books and magazines.
      Due to copyright restrictions, however, the images used in the final version of this site were generated specifically for this project using AI tools and are intended solely for non-commercial educational use as part of the WDD 131 course..

Selected web and institutional sources

  • Artesanías del Perú – Official handicrafts platform (Mincetur, Peru)
  • MyGuidePeru – Mercado Indio de Miraflores (crafts and shopping)
  • Andina – “La artesanía peruana tiene potencial para conquistar más mercados”
  • Ministerio de Cultura del Perú – Cultural heritage and traditional arts
  • Peru.travel – Official tourism portal of Peru (culture and handicrafts sections)
  • Smithsonian Center for Folklife and Cultural Heritage – Traditional arts resources
  • UNESCO – Intangible Cultural Heritage (traditional craftsmanship)
  • World Crafts Council – International craft promotion and documentation
  • El Comercio – Peruvian press articles on culture, textiles and regional crafts
  • Ministerio de Comercio Exterior y Turismo (Mincetur) – Tourism and handicrafts initiatives
  • Government of Peru – Official portals and documents on cultural and economic development
  • Additional books, academic articles, and catalogues from museums and artisan organizations were also consulted as general background on Peruvian handicrafts and regional traditions.

This website was created for educational purposes only as part of the BYU–Idaho WDD 131 course. It is not an official guide and should be used as a learning resource and design portfolio piece.
