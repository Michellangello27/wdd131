How to read this guide

This page introduces the main techniques used in Peruvian handicrafts. Each section explains the basic process, the traditional tools, and the cultural meaning behind the craft. Use this guide as a starting point to recognize authentic pieces and appreciate the work behind them.

Main techniques

Ceramics

Peruvian ceramics combine ancestral forms with contemporary design. Artisans work with local clay, shaping vessels, figurines, and tiles that preserve symbols from Nazca, Moche, and other ancient cultures.

  • Main materials: clay, mineral pigments, natural slips
  • Typical tools: simple wheels, carving sticks, polishing stones
  • Regions: Ayacucho, Cusco, Cajamarca

The basic process includes preparing the clay, shaping by hand or wheel, drying, decorating with slips or pigments, and firing in traditional or gas kilns.

Textiles

Andean textiles are known worldwide for their color, durability, and symbolic patterns. Many pieces are still woven on backstrap or pedal looms using techniques passed down for generations.

  • Main materials: alpaca wool, sheep wool, cotton
  • Typical tools: backstrap looms, shuttles, combs
  • Regions: Cusco, Puno, Junín

The process starts with shearing, washing, spinning, and dyeing the fibers—often with natural dyes—before warping the loom and weaving complex patterns row by row.

Wood carving

Wood carving in Peru ranges from religious imagery to masks and decorative panels. Artisans often start from a single block of wood and reveal the shape through patient, precise carving.

  • Main materials: local hardwoods, softwoods
  • Typical tools: chisels, gouges, knives, sanding tools
  • Regions: Cusco, Ayacucho, Amazonian areas

A design is sketched on the wood, then rough cuts define the volume. Details are carved gradually, followed by sanding, staining, and sometimes painting or gilding.

Silver filigree

Silver filigree is one of the most delicate Peruvian techniques. Fine silver wires are twisted and arranged into lace-like patterns to create earrings, pendants, and decorative elements.

  • Main materials: sterling silver wire
  • Typical tools: pliers, fine tweezers, soldering tools
  • Regions: Catacaos, Cusco, Lima

Artisans draw the design, form and twist the silver wires, then assemble and solder them with great care before polishing and finishing each piece by hand.
